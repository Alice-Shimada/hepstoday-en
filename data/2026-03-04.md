### [65] [Search for a narrow resonance with a mass between 10 and 70 GeV decaying to a pair of photons in proton-proton collisions at $\sqrt{s}$ = 13 TeV](https://arxiv.org/abs/2603.03250)
*CMS Collaboration*

Main category: hep-ex

TL;DR: CMS searches for low-mass spin-zero resonances (e.g., axion-like particles) decaying to photon pairs in 13 TeV pp collisions (54.4 fb⁻¹); no significant excess found; sets upper limits on σ(gg→X) × B(X→γγ) and interprets them in an ALP EFT framework.

Motivation: Theoretical models predict light spin-zero particles (e.g., axion-like particles) below the electroweak scale; LHC diphoton resonance searches provide a sensitive probe, especially at low mass where prior coverage was limited.

Method: Search for narrow resonances in the diphoton invariant mass spectrum (10–70 GeV) using CMS data from 2018 (13 TeV, 54.4 fb⁻¹), enabled by a new low-threshold diphoton trigger; background modeled with smooth analytic function; statistical inference via profile likelihood; limits computed using CLs method.

Result: No statistically significant excess observed over SM background expectation; 95% CL upper limits set on σ(gg→X) × B(X→γγ) across 10–70 GeV; interpreted as constraints on ALP couplings (e.g., gₐγγ, Λ) in dimension-5 effective Lagrangian.

Conclusion: This is the first CMS search optimized for sub-70 GeV diphoton resonances using the 2018 dataset; results improve existing constraints on light ALPs coupling to gluons and photons, excluding regions of ALP parameter space not previously probed at the LHC.

Abstract: The existence of a new spin-zero particle with a mass below the electroweak scale is predicted by several theoretical models. Searches for resonant production of photon pairs at the LHC are able to probe these models. We present a search for a narrow resonance produced through gluon fusion that decays into a pair of photons with an invariant mass between 10 and 70 GeV, using a proton-proton collision data set from the CMS experiment. This data set, corresponding to an integrated luminosity of 54.4 fb$^{-1}$, was recorded in 2018 at a center-of-mass energy of 13 TeV using a newly introduced diphoton trigger that enabled exploration of the low-mass diphoton spectrum. No significant excess above the expected background is observed. Upper limits are set on the product of the gluon fusion production cross section and the branching fraction of the diphoton decay of a narrow resonance. An interpretation of these limits within an effective field theory framework for axion-like particles is also provided.
