### [1] [Domain Walls from $Σ(36 \times 3)$, $Δ(54)$ and $Δ(27)$ potentials](https://arxiv.org/abs/2603.04496)
*Gonçalo Barreto,Ivo de Medeiros Varzielas,Ye-Ling Zhou*

Main category: hep-ph

TL;DR: This paper classifies domain walls and computes their tensions in scalar field theories with degenerate vacua arising from potentials invariant under discrete flavor symmetries Σ(36×3), Δ(54), and Δ(27), with or without CP.


<details>
  <summary>Details</summary>
Motivation: To systematically characterize topological defects—specifically domain walls—arising from spontaneous symmetry breaking in models with discrete non-Abelian flavor symmetries commonly used in neutrino physics and beyond-Standard-Model model building.

Method: Analyzes the vacuum structure of scalar potentials invariant under Σ(36×3) and its subgroups Δ(54) and Δ(27); identifies all physically distinct degenerate minima; constructs kink solutions interpolating between them; computes domain wall tensions via the standard Bogomol'nyi bound and numerical integration where needed.

Result: Complete classification of topologically distinct domain walls for each symmetry group (with/without CP); explicit analytical and numerical values for domain wall tensions; identification of stable vs. unstable (or metastable) walls based on tension hierarchy and CP properties.

Conclusion: Domain wall spectra are highly sensitive to the specific discrete symmetry and whether CP is imposed; results provide essential input for cosmological viability assessments and model selection in flavor-symmetric extensions of the Standard Model.

Abstract: We consider the degenerate minima arising from scalar potentials invariant under $Σ(36\times 3)$, or under its subgroups $Δ(54)$ and $Δ(27)$ (with or without imposed CP symmetries), for a triplet of those symmetries. In this framework, we classify the distinct Domain Walls between the degenerate minima and calculate the respective tensions.

### [2] [Momentum fraction and hard scale dependence of double parton scattering in heavy-ion collisions](https://arxiv.org/abs/2603.04510)
*Joao Vitor C. Lovato,Edgar Huayra,Emmanuel G. de Oliveira*

Main category: hep-ph

TL;DR: This paper extends double parton scattering (DPS) analysis from pp to pA and AA collisions, incorporating nuclear effects—shadowing, antishadowing, and a novel model for nuclear transverse parton distributions—revealing DPS as a probe of proton/nucleon transverse structure and nuclear geometry.


<details>
  <summary>Details</summary>
Motivation: To extend prior DPS studies in pp collisions to heavy-ion (pA and AA) systems and investigate how nuclear modifications—particularly shadowing, antishadowing, and altered transverse parton distributions—affect the effective DPS cross section and its observability.

Method: Incorporates nuclear PDFs with shadowing/antishadowing into DPS factorization; proposes a simple model for nuclear transverse parton distribution (TPD), assuming increased parton separation in bound nucleons; computes effective cross section σ_eff for pPb data and makes LHC predictions.

Result: Good agreement with existing pPb DPS data; predictions show final-state-dependent σ_eff, indicating sensitivity to transverse profiles: free proton and bound nucleon (dominant in pA), and full nucleus (dominant in AA).

Conclusion: DPS in heavy-ion collisions serves as a unique probe of transverse hadronic and nuclear structure, with pA collisions constraining nucleon TPD modifications and AA collisions probing nuclear geometry.

Abstract: In a previous work, we studied the momentum fraction and hard--scale dependence of double parton scattering (DPS) in proton--proton collisions and the resulting dependence of the effective cross section on the final--state observables. In this paper, we extend those results to heavy--ion ($pA$ and $AA$) collisions, accounting for nuclear effects in the relevant kinematic region, namely shadowing and antishadowing. In addition to modifying the longitudinal parton distributions, these effects also alter the transverse parton distribution of the nucleus, for which we propose a simple model. We further hypothesize that partons inside a bound nucleon are more widely separated than in a free proton. We compute the effective cross section for the available $p$Pb data, obtaining reasonable agreement, and provide predictions for future measurements at the LHC. The observed dependence of our predictions on the final state indicates that DPS in heavy--ion collisions can be used to probe the transverse profile of the free proton and the bound nucleon, primarily in $pA$ collisions, as well as the transverse structure of the nucleus, mainly in $AA$ collisions.

</details>
